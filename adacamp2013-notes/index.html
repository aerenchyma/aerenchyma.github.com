Adacamp2013-notes

Jun 10th, 2013

tl;dr intro

AdaCamp is explained here at this link.

I went to a few sessions, namely: Open Education, Open Source in Education, From Syntax to Coding, and Ada Lovelace: the Mythos + read notes from several others.

I’m hoping to put things I learned together in a more structured/concise narrative, but for the moment, in an effort not to logjam things because I need them to be perfect/presentable, I put together some notes as I took them that seemed particularly relevant to things I’ve been recently doing or thinking about that also seem relevant to Open.Michigan in at least a tangential way (this isn’t to say all of this is immediately relevant by any means)

Some of this time in sessions, etc, was spent split up into smaller groups for discussion, so some of these notes are direct from notes that other people took as summaries.

Comments at AdaCamp are not attributed to people without explicit consent, by agreement, hence total lack of participant names, etc.

I’ve tried to split things up into approximate categories, but there is a a lot of crossover.

Idea-focused discussion, current efforts wrt ‘open education’

Discussion of WikiSeat – chairs are super important for education! –> open source framework for building chairs: opened tool, also an open source furniture design framework

  1. WikiSeat will weld together stuff for free
  2. they’ll send it to teachers, who give them to students
  3. teachers teach a few basics about design and where to start
  4. kids/students (all ages 4th grade - seniors in college) can take it home, build it on their own

This can work with mult curriculums, science classes, english classes, etc; aligned with lots of core standards

In general: education is not just what we may canoncially think of What plays in most integrally; assumption that open involvement is positive

People to look up with respect to this:

  • Antonio Von Drammond [British Columbia]
  • Sean Wheeler – charter school in Ohio; a first makerschool (Lakewood, OH) – probably 2014
  • teachinghumans.com ^^

Overall, great example of openness and remixing for education that could help - broaden the/a conversation - useful as an example - more? I think probably more, but have to think about this in more detail

In education systems teachers often actively hostile towards open information

If you ask your average middle school teacher is Wikipedia accurate? They will say No!

  • Academics are only credited based on publishing in journals with the highest prestige
  • The open source journals aren’t ranked as highly
  • Your merit is valued higher if you publish in certain journals
  • Two camps within open access, about open access [ more detail on this?? ]
  • Some people think Public Library of Science is great, others think they will publish anything

The community is heavily made up of silos, even sub-silos (coding, “people work”)

  • Is it possible for a focus on open culture/culture of sharing to HELP SOLVE this? Probably?
  • Yet it’s hard if you trust (your coworkers, your community, your partners) – hard then to trust other systems, especially enough to move to them 100%
    • plus the tech system problems and how they diverge
    • in open source: how can you make a generalizable system that will help More People than a single community? can one make such a thing that people may actually want to use enough that they will? are there enough resources for learning new systems without huge time investment?
    • and what is the priority of these umbrella questions?
