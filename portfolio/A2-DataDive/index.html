A2 Data Dive

The A2DataDive was founded in 2011 with inspiration (and assistance!) from DataKind; it is a hackathon that treats data science as a service.

I served as the Data Ambassador for the African Health OER Network (AHON) organization client at the first A2DataDive in February 2012, returned to Ann Arbor from Boston to be at the 2nd A2DataDive in February 2013, where I worked with the team supporting the Huron River Watershed Council (HRWC); some of our results can be seen here, in a redesigned database schema. I served as a Data Ambassador again for the 3rd A2DataDive, working with the Ann Arbor YMCA, which occurred on the weekend of November 9th, 2013. Results of the weekend included several d3.js visualizations of the data, iPython notebook charts using the Python library Pandas data analysis library, and statistical analysis which discovered, among other things, that a 1% increase in temperature predicted a 2% increase in membership of the YMCA for the month.

I coordinated the 4th A2 Data Dive with three other School of Information Masters students, retooling the event with a stronger focus on peer education and celebration of communication about data; my role was managing the Data Ambassador teams who carried the day by communication with client contacts and facilitation of individual teams on the big event weekend. We have turned the A2 Data Dive from a weekend event to a year-long series of events focused on data literacy and understanding from a big variety of perspectives.

More about the current and past A2 Data Dives can be found on the website.
