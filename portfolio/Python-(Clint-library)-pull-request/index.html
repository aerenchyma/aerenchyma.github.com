Python Clint library fun

While I was at Hacker School, in the summer of 2012, our whole cohort of the workshop participated in something we called Open Source Week, when we focused the entire week on learning about (F)OSS projects and contributing to them. I spent the first part of the week learning about Socorro thanks to Laura Thomson and Brandon Savage from Mozilla.

Later in the week, my friend Tom Ballinger, a Hacker School facilitator, and I came upon this issue in the Clint (Command Line Application Tools) library by kennethreitz and submitted this pull request, which was soon merged.

It was a fairly quick and an enjoyable low-priority bug to solve and to play with, and it got me thinking a lot about what I really love about programming, especially the learning process itself. What does it mean that something is structured the way it is? What is the expected behavior of a library, especially a library intended to supplement command line functionality? What do the internals of getattr() in Python really look like? And what’s the best way to explain these things to a group of people who haven’t yet encountered them? Fun questions.
